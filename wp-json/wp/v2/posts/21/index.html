India missed the chances that came their way to settle for a goalless draw with Lebanon in the Intercontinental Cup football tournament here on Thursday.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Continuing with his policy to make wholesome changes to his starting XI, head coach Igor Stimac benched a number of seniors including striker Sunil Chhetri and goalkeeper Gurpreet Singh Sandhu.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Defensive linchpin Sandesh Jhingan wore the captain\u2019s armband in Chhetri\u2019s absence.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The result however did not matter as both teams had already sealed their places in the final of the four-nation tournament. Mongolia and Vanuatu were the two other teams taking part in the event.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Introduced in the 82nd minute, Chhetri received a perfect delivery from Anirudh Thapa but the India captain could not find the back of the net.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

It would have been 1-0 India within the first five minutes of the game but, after being set up by Lallianzuala Chhangte and with just the opposition goalkeeper to beat, Anirudh Thapa missed a sitter on the fourth minute. All he could do was shoot the ball away.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Then, almost 20 minutes into the match, another chance was wasted as Ashique Kuruniyan hit the ball straight to the Lebanese goalkeeper after a good ball inside the box.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The two missed chances from India meant the teams went into the half-time break goalless.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

It pretty much continued in the same way in the second half, which also saw the floodlights going off for a couple of minutes, as both teams failed to failed to find the target.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Hoping to break the deadlock, both India and Lebanon rang in a few changes, but to no avail as the score remained unchanged till the final whistle.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
